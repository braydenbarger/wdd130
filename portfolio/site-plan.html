Content

Home page

Introduction: I am Brayden Barger, studying computer science at BYU-Idaho. This website showcases the work I’ve done in my Web Development course, including projects and applied concepts, aimed at future employers and anyone interested in my web development skills.

Audience: The site is designed for potential employers, instructors, and peers who want to view my portfolio and web development skills. It’s also a way for me to contribute to the broader web development community.

What Visitors Can Expect: Visitors will find sections with projects I’ve completed, concepts I’ve applied, and an easy way to contact me. Each section highlights different skills, allowing viewers to see a range of work and techniques.

Images for the Home page

Websites

Projects Overview: This section showcases various projects completed during my course, covering skills like HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and responsive design. Each project demonstrates practical applications of what I’ve learned.

Skills and Technologies Used: In these projects, I used tools like HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and frameworks like Bootstrap to create responsive, interactive web pages. Each project helps to show my ability to build and troubleshoot.

Why Visitors Will Come to the Projects Page: This section will help employers and instructors evaluate my technical skills and project completion. It also offers inspiration to other students or developers working on similar projects.

Images for Page 2

Concept Practices

Concept Practices Overview: In this section, I display my knowledge of important concepts like user experience (UX), responsive design, and clean code structure. These practices are reflected in my projects, showing how each concept improves the final product.

Benefits of Learning and Applying Concepts: Learning and applying concepts like UX, accessibility, and responsive design has made my projects more professional and user-friendly. This shows visitors my dedication to continuous improvement and understanding industry standards.

Why Visitors Will Come to the Concept Practices Page: This page is especially valuable for employers and instructors looking to assess my practical understanding of core web development concepts. It also provides a resource for other students learning these same principles.
